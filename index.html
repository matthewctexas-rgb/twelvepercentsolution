How does the 12% Solution Work?

Carter's 12% Solution entails capturing market momentum by analyzing and buying the highest performing ETF over the past three months. Switching once a month mutes misleading market noise while still producing growth.

The Equity Sector Rotation (60%)

At the end of every calendar month, analyze the 3-month performance (momentum) of four broad index ETFs: SPY, IWM, MDY, QQQ, and determine the one with the highest gain. If its gain is positive compared to SHY (the cash proxy), allocate 60% of your total capital here.

Core Index ETFs SPY, IWM, MDY, QQQ

Safe-Haven Bond Allocation (40%)

The remaining 40% is kept safe within bonds. Similar to with the stocks, compare the 3-month returns of long-term Treasuries (TLT) and high-yield junk bonds (JNK), and invest in the outperformer. Bond allocation offers protection and/or secondary growth according to current interest rate climates.

Hedge Bond ETFs TLT, JNK

The Protective Cash Trigger

During severe macro economic depressions (like 2008), high-yield indexes often fall below short-term Treasuries. Therefore, if all four equity ETFs show negative 3-month returns or return less than the SHY cash proxy, the strategy triggers an immediate escape. The 60% equity portion rotates safely into SHY.

Cash Proxy Instrument SHY (Short-term Treasury)
